PREPARATION

Essentials:
What are the entry requirements (visas, permits, vaccinations...)
Is the country/region safe to visit? (heck tavel advisory pages like ... if not sure)
What is the current situation (holidays, elections, unrest)
What weather can you expect? Will you be at a high altitude?
If going to multiple countries, how will you cross the borders? Are land crossings open to tourists? Are the flights expensive?
How will you handle money? Can you use local ATMs?

WAYS TO TRAVEL

1. Luxury tourist/family:
  • Transport: Flight + rented car or tour bus
  • Accommodation: Resort or hotel
  • Food: At the resort/hotel
  • Places to see: Stay in resort or hire a guide to visit famous sights
  • What to pack: Whatever you want
  • Experience: Enjoy the luxury but learn nothing about local life. In poor countries, your money can have a big impact. Make sure it's mostly good one (read more about responsible travel...)
  • Budget: 500-∞ $/week
2. Independent backpacker:
  • Transport: Flight + public transport. Sometimes rent a motorbike or bicycle. Private taxi only as a last resort. (Read my advice here)
  • Accommodation: Hostel or budget hotel. Sometimes stay with friends or couchsurfing (My advice on finding accommodation)
  • Food: local restaurant or market
  • Places to see: Anywhere accessible by road (my recommendations)
  • What to pack: One carry-on bag. Don't bring any camping gear unless you are sure you will use it. (my packing suggestions)
  • Experience: Depends on your attitude and your luck. (My advice)
  • Budget: 200-600 $/week (My advice on handling money)
3. Adventurer/explorer/homeless:
  • Transport: Walk/hitchhike/bicycle/horesback/kayak/etc.
  • Accommodation: Camping or couchsurfing. Sleep on a bus.
  • Food: local market, dumpster, cook by yourself
  • Places to see: Anywhere
  • What to pack: Ideally, one carry-on bag, but can be difficult if camping in cold climate. Pack as light as possible. Consider bringing a bivy/hammock/tarp instead of a tent to reduce the weight. (my advice on camping)
  • Experience: You will suffer but you will see places and have experiences that are unattainable to other travelers.
  • Budget: 0-300 $/week
All of these can be enjoyable in their own way, although many people can't afford #1 or #2. #3 can be a great option for both rich and poor people, but you need to be relatively fit and in good health.
