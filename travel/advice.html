PREPARATION

Essentials:
What are the entry requirements (visas, permits, vaccinations...)
Is the country/region safe to visit? (heck tavel advisory pages like ... if not sure)
What is the current situation (holidays, elections, unrest)
What weather can you expect? Will you be at a high altitude?
If going to multiple countries, how will you cross the borders? Are land crossings open to tourists? Are the flights expensive?
How will you handle money? Can you use local ATMs?

HOW TO TRAVEL
3 main ways to travel:
1. Luxury tourist/family:
Transport: Flight + rented car or tour bus
Accommodation: Resort or hotel (agoda.com)
Food: At the resort/hotel
Places to see: Stay in resort or hire a guide to visit famous sights
What to pack: Whatever you want
Experience: Enjoy the luxury but learn nothing about local life
Budget: 500-1million $/week

2. Independent backpacker:
Transport: Flight + public transport. Sometimes rent a motorbike. Private taxi only as a last resort.
Accommodation: Hostel or budget hotel (agoda.com). Sometimes stay with friends or couchsurfing
Food: local restaurant or market
Places to see: Anywhere accessible by road
What to pack: One carry-on bag. Don't bring any camping gear unless you are sure you will use it.
Experience: Depends on your attitude and your luck.
Budget: 200-500 $/week

3. Adventurer/explorer/homeless:
Transport: Walk, hitchhike, cycle, public transport.
Accommodation: Camping or couchsurfing. Sleep on a bus.
Food: local market, cook by yourself
Places to see: Anywhere
What to pack: Ideally, one carry-on bag, but can be difficult in cold climate. Lightweight gear is essential. Consider bringing a bivy/hammock/tarp instead of a ten to reduce the weight.
Experience: See places other travelers will never get to visit.
Budget: 0-300 $/week
