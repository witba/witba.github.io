OVERLAND TRANSPORT


BUS/MINIVAN
Learn the system for the country you're in. Sometimes you pay online, sometimes at the ticket office, sometimes to the driver, sometimes to the conductor.
Sometimes there is a main bus station, sometimes the buses are scattered around the city. Usually you can hail down buses as they pass by.
Sometimes there is a schedule, sometimes the buses only leave when full.
Sometimes the drivers are competing with each other. They will rush you to their bus/minivan and then you have to wait an hour for it to fill up with passengers. Get inside buses that look almost full. If there are no passengers inside then it is unlikely to leave soon.
Negotiate the price and don't get overcharged. Ask other passengers what the price is.

TRAIN
For long distance, you sometimes need to get a ticket in advance.
Sleep on the train or bus to save money on accommodation.

SHARED TAXI
In some countries, it is a common form of public transport. It's like hitchhiking but you're expected to pay. Simply wave down a passing car or tricycle and say where you're going. Sometimes there is a fixed fare but usually you have to negotiate the price. The driver will make stops on the way to pick up more passengers.

TAXI/MOTORBIKE TAXI
Always negotiate the price before you get in.
Never put your bags in the trunk.
Don't trust the drivers. They will try to scam you or sell you something. Better stick to public transport or walking or use ride-hailng apps.

HITCHHIKING
Wait on intersections or places where cars need to slow down. Make sure there is enough space for a car to stop.
It is often better to keep walking down the road rather than waiting in one spot. It will signal the drivers that you're not a lazy bum but someone putting in effort. You might even reach another intersection with better traffic.
Be friendly and ready to chat with the drivers, they are often looking for a good conversation.
Expect to wait much longer if you are two or more people, especially if you're male.
Don't get in the car if you have a bad feeling about the driver. Make up some excuse (e.g. "I need to wait for my friend").
In some countries you shouldn't use the thumb gesture.

FLIGHTS
Buying tickets early doesn't necessarily get you a better price. Sometimes buying a few weeks or days before the flight can be better. Different airlines will have special offers at different times.
To get the lowest price, be flexibe with the date you are flying and check the prices frequently. Look for alternative connections with multile stop-overs and search these legs separately.
