OVERLAND TRANSPORT


BUS/MINIVAN
Learn the system for the country you visit. Sometimes you pay online, sometimes at the ticket office, sometimes to the driver, sometimes to the conductor.
Sometimes there is a main bus station, sometimes the buses are scattered around the city. Usually you can hail down buses as they pass by.
Sometimes there is a schedule, sometimes the buses leave when full.
Sometimes the bus drivers are competing with each other. They will rush you to their bus and then you have to wait one hour for it to fill up with passengers. Get inside buses that look almost full. If there are no passengers inside then it is unlikely to leave anytime soon.
Negotiate the price and don't get overcharged. Ask other passengers what the price is.

TRAIN
For long distance, you sometimes need to get a ticket in advance.

SHARED TAXI
In some countries, it is a common form of public transport. IT's like hitchhiking but you're expected to pay. Simply wave down a passing car or tricycle and say where you're going. Sometimes there is a fixed fare but usually you have to negotiate the price. The driver will make stops to pick up more passengers.

TAXI/MOTORBIKE TAXI
Always negotiate the price before you get in.
Never put your bags in the trunk.
Don't trust the drivers. They will try to scam you or sell you something. Stick to public transport or walking or use ride-hailng apps.

HITCHHIKING
In some countries you shouldn't use the thumb gesture.
Wait on intersections or places where cars need to slow down. Make sure there is enough space for a car to stop.
It is often better to keep walking down the road rather than waiting in one spot. It will signal the drivers that you're not a lazy bum but someone putting in effort. You might even reach another intersection with better traffic.
Be friendly and ready to chat to the drivers, they are often looking for a good conversation.
Expect to wait much longer if you are two or more people, especially if male.
Don't get in the car if you have a bad feeling about the driver. Make up some excuse (e.g. "I need to wait for my friend").
FLIGHTS
Buying tickets early doesn't necessarily get you a better price. Sometimes buying a few wees or days before the flight, when the airlines need to fill empty seats can be better. Different airlines will have special offers at different times.
To get the lowest price, be flexibe with the date you are flying and check the prices frequently. Look for alternative connections with multile stopovers and search these legs separately.
