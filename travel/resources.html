Useful websites:
Wikitravel and it's fork Wikivoyage: Free online guides
Thorn Tree forum: Travel forum
UNESCO World Heritage Sites (a more detailed map here)
Sightsmap: Excellent for discovering places to see, especially ones that are not marked on maps.
Climate Maps: Monthly climate maps (also here)
Summitpost: Hiking and climbing
Opentopomap: Topographic maps
Hikebikemap: Maps of hiking trails
Loadmap: Old Soviet military maps, often more detailed than modern ones. More maps here but less user-friendly.
Openbusmap: Maps of bus and train routes
Hitchwiki: Hitchhiking guide. See also their map of hitchhiking locations
Berkeley Earth's Air Quality map: Air pollution can badly affect your trip
Visa requirements on Wikipedia: See your country's page for a list of countries for which you need a visa
Travelpod blogs were the best place to find info about obscure places. Sadly, the website got bought and shut down by Tripadvisor. Please boycott this awful company.
Library Genesis: For finding guidebook PDFs.

Transport/accommodation:
Agoda: Accommodation
Booking: Accommodation
Hostelworld: Accommodation
Google: Accommodation
Trustroots: Hospitality exchange
Couchsurfing: Hospitality exchange
Rome2rio: Find bus, train, and ferry connections
Seat 61: Information about train travel with pictures, schedules, fares and links to ticket websites
Flightconnections: For planning flight routes (used to be free to use, now it seems you need to pay)
Skyscanner: Flights search engine
Kayak: Flights search engine
Momondo: Flights search engine
Azair: Flights search engine with very customizable search functions (low-cost airlines)
