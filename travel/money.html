MONEY: BEFORE YOU LEAVE
  • Find a bank that does not charge for international transactions or foreign ATM withdrawals. ING, Citibank in Australia, many credit unions in USA.
  • Traveler cheques, travel cards, etc. are probably not worth getting because of exchange rates and because you will have unspent money after your trip.
  • Exchanging cash in a western country will likely get you terrible rates, especially at airports. It's better to exchange money in non-western countries. USD and Euro usually get the best rates. Beware of scams and hidden commissions, always count your money.
HANDLING MONEY
  • Avoid traveling with a lot of cash.
  • Don't keep your valuables visible and remember where you put them. Although generally it is good practice to hidde your valuables in several places, this makes it easier to lose something.
  • Keep coins and small bills for daily expenses seperate from large bills and other valuables (e.g. in another pocket).
  • It is probably safer to leave valuable items at the hotel than to always carry them with you.
  • Don't carry any large money in a wallet. Wallets are easily spotted by thieves.
  • Keep some emergency cash somewhere well hidden, in case you get robbed or can't find a working ATM. US dollar is easy to exchange anywhere in the world.
  • Some foreign ATMs will ask if you wish to be charged in local currency or in your bank's currency. Always choose local currency to avoid rip-off rates.
  • Pay with cash. You can easily have card details stolen.
Some tips on saving money:
  • To have money for travel, focus on saving it in expensive countries, not in cheap countries (especially if you're someone who spends 7$ for a beer back home). It's good for your wallet and for global equality. In a poor country your money can do a lot more good, both for you and for local people, than in a rich country.
  • Avoid staying in touristy areas.
  • If you live in an expensive city, renting out your room while you're away can often save enough money to cover your entire trip.
