MONEY: BEFORE YOU LEAVE
  • Find a bank that does not charge for international transactions or ATM withdrawals. ING, Citibank in Australia, many credit unions in the USA.
  • Exchanging cash in a western country will get you terrible rates, especially at airports. It's better to exchange money in non-western countries. Beware of scams and hidden commissions, always count your money. US dollar is the most widely accepted currency.
HANDLING MONEY
  • Keep coins and small bills for daily expenses seperate from large bills and other valuables (e.g. in a different pocket).
  • Don't carry any large money in a wallet. Wallets are easily spotted by thieves.
  • In general, it is safer to leave valuable items at the hotel than to carry them with you.
  • Keep some emergency cash somewhere well hidden, just in case.
  • Some ATMs will ask if you wish to be charged in local currency or in your bank's currency. Always choose local currency to avoid rip-off rates.
Some tips on saving money:
  • To have money for travel, focus on saving it in expensive countries, not in cheap countries (especially if you're someone who spends 7$ for a beer back home). It's good for your wallet and for global equality. In a poor country your money can do a lot more good, both for you and for local people, than in a rich country.
  • Avoid staying in touristy areas.
  • If you live in an expensive city, renting out your room while you're away can often save enough money to cover all your trip expenses.
  • If you are a student, a teacher, or under 31, get an ISIC/ITIC/IYTC card. It includes travel insurance and some discounts. Costs about 30$.
